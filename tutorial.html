Blender Tutorial

Here is a detailed list of steps I took to produce three difference map scenes from Blender, one still and two animations. References are listed at the end.

Setup

Created in Blender v 2.91.1 on Windows 10.

Large-Scale Ocean Construction

I followed Dylan Neill's ocean tutorial on YouTube closely to produce the ocean in my scenes. I will not re-state his steps, but begin my tutorial assuming an ocean constructed from his tutorial. Changes to ocean preview bake:

1. Adjust the frame end value depending on how long you'd like your animation to be. In my case, I tried 60fps for 900 frames (starting at 1 and ending with 901), for a 15 second animation. I also played with 30fps, 450 frames for a quicker render. 2. Re-bake your ocean preview. Unless you're happy with Neill's bake settings, you'll have to specify a new cache and re-bake your ocean preview with the correct number of frames.
3. Change keyframes to accommodate new framerate/time of animation. You'll have irregular or repeating loops in your animation if you don't change this. Changes to ocean/background:
1. Experiment with background strengths until you find something you like. For me, this was 0.800.
2. Change the source of baked frames in the ocean object shader editor. Choose the cache where you stored your new baked ocean preview. Also change the number of frames to match your animation.

Adding a Landmass

This is where my original work begins. Once you have the ocean set up however you like, you will need some sort of landmass to add to it (otherwise this tutorial is not going to be helpful). Creating your landmass:

1. Create a mesh plane. Click on the scene in Object Mode and using shift+a create a mesh plane in its own Collection.
2. Scale your plane to fit the ocean in Object Properties. For me this was ~50x for X, Y, Z. Make sure that whatever scale you decide on is the same for X, Y, Z to eliminate distortion. 2. Re-bake your ocean preview. Unless you're happy with Neill's bake settings, you'll have to specify a new cache and re-bake your ocean preview with the correct number of frames. 3. Change keyframes to accommodate new framerate/time of animation. You'll have irregular or repeating loops in your animation if you don't change this. Changes to ocean/background:
1. Experiment with background strengths until you find something you like. For me, this was 0.800.
2. Change the source of baked frames in the ocean object shader editor. Choose the cache where you stored your new baked ocean preview. Also change the number of frames to match your animation.

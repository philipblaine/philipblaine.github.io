Mauna Kea

In this project I combined knowledge from our cartography courses with self-taught techniques in Blender. I have created a top-down hillshade of Hawaii, an oblique 3D still image comparing Mauna Kea to Mount Everest, and two 3D animations of Hawaii and Mauna Kea. All of these were first rendered in Blender, and the top-down hillshade was then touched up in Photoshop and labels added in Illustrator. I recommend viewing the animations at maximum YouTube video quality. Animation tutorial below!

Hawaiian Giants

This hillshade of the island of Hawaii intentionally depicts the scene without an ocean. As the tallest mountain in the world, I wanted to showcase the elevation landscape surrounding Mauna Kea. While most know Mount Everest as the highest mountain, few are aware of Mauna Kea's size. I chose not to include any land cover layers because the emphasis of this map is the elevation of Hawaiian mountains and their related features. Elevations here are listed as from the sea floor. One can surmise a rough outline of the coast of Hawaii by observing the coastal cities. This map can be used as reference when viewing the animations below!

Data Sources

Collection created by Philip Meersman for GEO405 Spring 2021. Bathymetery and DEM data from NOAA Bathymetric Data Viewer. While elevation information is true, visual sizes of landforms were exaggerated for effect. Aerial imagery from Bing Maps. Projection is a Pseudo-Mercator default from QGIS.
